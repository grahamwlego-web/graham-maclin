What's Next for
Super Kitty & Ginger

By Graham Maclin  ·  January 10, 2026

When I first wrote Super Kitty and Good Dog Ginger, I didn't know how far the story would go. A rap-a-tap-tap on a window. An urgent message. A kidnapping. And then — a sparkling white lab, an injection called SSI, and two heroes who wake up with no idea what's been done to them.

That ending always felt like a beginning to me. And I think it's time to find out what SSI actually does.

Eye, Pee, and Bow Incorporated

The three cats — one with an eye patch, one with a bowtie, one yellow — are still out there. The building blew up, yes. But a drone caught them escaping. They are very much alive, and they are very much still planning something.

What do they want? Why did they inject Kitty and Ginger? And what does SSI stand for? Those are the questions that are going to drive the next part of this story.

"Kitty woke up floating above a Taco Bell. That felt like a clue."

Kitty can fly

Let's talk about what we know. At the end of the book, Kitty wakes up floating above a Taco Bell — which then explodes in a fireball of what she describes as "nasty looking tacos." She can move through the air. She can run through the sky at midnight.

The SSI injection did something to her. And whatever it did, Eye Cat's gang is going to want it back.

Ginger's aim

Let's not forget that Ginger took out an armed attacker with a frozen pizza. A frozen pizza. That kind of quick thinking is exactly what's going to be needed when Eye Cat makes his next move.

I'm very excited about where these two are headed. Stay tuned — the adventure is just getting started.
